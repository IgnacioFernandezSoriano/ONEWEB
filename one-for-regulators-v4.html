Your Regulatory Mission

Four objectives.
One foundation.

Every postal regulator shares the same mandate. Meeting it requires one thing: objective, independent, credible data that every stakeholder accepts without dispute.

A single foundation underpins all four objectives: objective, independent, and credible data — a methodology accepted by governments, operators, and consumers worldwide.

Objective · Independent · Credible
The three pillars of data that regulators, operators and consumers all accept.
01
Quality of Service Control
Verify operator compliance with service standards and take corrective action when they fall short.
02
System Quality Improvement
Support Designated Operators in identifying performance gaps and driving improvements across the system.
03
Consumer Protection
Resolve complaints with credible evidence. Ensure transparent public reporting and nationwide coverage.
04
Licence Management
Evaluate operators objectively for licence renewals and set clear, enforceable operating conditions.
How ONE Addresses the Challenges

Three challenges.
Three structural solutions.

One UN-endorsed platform purpose-built for regulatory independence.

01
Regulators need a real-time view of network performance — not just historical reports — to act proactively.
End-to-End & Network Visibility

Through sampling or real postal events, ONE provides a near real-time view of the entire postal journey — from pick-up to final delivery.

02
Relying on a single data source — often the operator itself — undermines the independence regulators need.
Independent & Verifiable Data

An independent panel of senders with automatic EDI and RFID capture generates data that is unbiased, verifiable, and accepted by all parties.

03
Without a common framework, comparing operators or benchmarking over time is unreliable — and legally contestable.
A UPU-Endorsed Global Standard

The UPU provides a single, consistent methodology recognised by governments, operators, and consumers — built on 15+ years of measurement experience.

Choose based on your regulatory objectives, budget, and network complexity.

End-to-End Measurement

Measures total transit time between two cities for a specific carrier and product — the core layer of regulatory oversight and the foundation for compliance reporting.

Definition
Total transit time between two cities for a specific carrier and product.
Method
Independent panelists or barcode scanning register dispatch and receipt times. Multi-carrier, multi-product: letters, parcels, express, registered mail.
Outcome
Verified compliance with delivery standards. Evidence base for regulatory action, tariff decisions, and licence management.

Origin panelist — City A
An independent panelist dispatches a real test item (letter, parcel, or registered mail) from City A using the operator's normal service. The injection timestamp (T inject) is recorded automatically via the ONE Agent App. The panelist is fully independent — not affiliated with the operator. → Panelist Management · Agent App · Allocation Plan
The postal network — a measured black box
E2E does not instrument the internal network. The sorting centres, transport legs, and delivery offices between City A and City B are not individually tracked. What matters is the total transit time. If performance fails, Network Diagnostic opens this box to find exactly where. → E2E Events DB · Compliance Report · Territory Equity Report
Destination panelist — City B
A second independent panelist in City B receives the item and confirms delivery via the app. The delivery timestamp (T delivery) is recorded. The system calculates: T delivery − T inject = E2E transit time. This is compared against the J+K target to determine compliance. → J+K Performance Report · Regulatory Compliance Report

Network Diagnostic Measurement

Extends E2E by measuring intermediate points throughout the postal network. Identifies where delays occur — not just that they occur.

Definition
Extends E2E by measuring intermediate points — sorting centres, transport legs, and post offices.
Method
RFID tags and antennas at facility entry/exit gates. Hundreds of reads per second. Fully automated and unassisted.
Outcome
Identification of bottlenecks, network black spots, best practices, and efficiency leaders across the entire postal network.
Cost-Optimisation: Rotating Measurement Scheme (RMS)
A single RFID reader rotates across multiple facility locations on a scheduled cycle — capturing representative network data while significantly reducing capital expenditure.

Origin Sorting Centre — RFID readers at entry and exit gates capture the exact time each item spends inside this facility. The system measures: time from arrival → processing → departure. Compared across all routes and centres to identify operational outliers. → Postal Centre Diagnostic · Centre Efficiency Ranking
Intermediate Sorting Centre — Same automated RFID capture. Intermediate centres are often hidden bottlenecks — items may arrive on time but accumulate delays here before onward transport. The Diagnostic isolates this invisible delay. → Network Black Spots Report · Centre Efficiency Ranking
Destination Sorting Centre — Final sorting before last-mile delivery. Delays here directly cause consumer-facing failures. The Diagnostic identifies whether the issue is the centre's own processing or the transport arriving late. → Postal Centre Diagnostic · Territory Equity Report
Transport Leg 1 — The time between departure from the Origin Centre and arrival at the Intermediate Centre. RFID captures the exact loading/unloading timestamps. Unreliable transport is the most common cause of E2E failures — and the hardest to detect without Diagnostic. → Transport Reliability Analysis · Network Black Spots
Transport Leg 2 — Same capture between Intermediate and Destination centres. The system builds a reliability profile for every transport link: on-time rate, average delay, variance. Green Lanes (best performers) and Black Spots (worst) are ranked automatically. → Transport Reliability Analysis · Green Lanes Report
